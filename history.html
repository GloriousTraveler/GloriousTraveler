Cameron Highlands is located in the state of Pahang, Malaysia. It is a popular hill station situated in the Titiwangsa Mountains, about 200 kilometers north of Kuala Lumpur and roughly 90 kilometers from Ipoh. The area is known for its cool climate, tea plantations, strawberry farms, and scenic beauty.

Cameron Highlands in Pahang, Malaysia, has a history dating back to the late 19th century. British surveyor William Cameron first explored the area in 1885, but serious development began in 1926 when it was established as a hill station. Tea plantations like Boh Tea Plantation flourished in the cool climate, alongside farms growing vegetables and flowers. After World War II, it became a popular tourist spot and agricultural hub, with attractions such as strawberry farms, flower nurseries, and the unique Mossy Forest. Despite its popularity, the region faces challenges like deforestation and traffic congestion due to rapid development. Cameron Highlands today blends its colonial heritage with modern attractions, offering visitors a refreshing getaway and insights into Malaysia's agricultural history..
